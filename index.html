Hello there, and welcome to my homepage!

My name is Aritra Chakrabarty, and I am an Indian Astrophysicist specializing in exoplanet research. I am currently a NASA Postdoctoral Program (NPP) Fellow working at NASA Ames Research Center.

My current research centers on the demographics of small exoplanets, including the search for potential Earth 2.0 candidates. I work extensively with Kepler mission data, refining the pipeline and reanalyzing raw data with the core mission team. This enhanced approach targets the mission's sensitive regimes and edge cases, aiming to recover additional Earth-sized planets in habitable zonesâ€”regions where liquid water could exist. I also work with theories of planet formation and evolution as well as population models, seeking to understand how current statistics of exoplanets can inform us of how they formed and evolved in the past. Fun fact: I also explore the possibility of the existence of water worlds!

Additionally, I develop and work with atmospheric and radiative transfer models to generate captivating simulations of spectral and polarization signals emanating from exoplanets. This knowledge opens pathways to probe the atmospheres of these distant worlds by leveraging advances in observational technology.

All these works demand advanced statistical modeling, numerical simulations, and cutting-edge data processing techniques to push the boundaries of our understanding of the distant worlds.

Visit the research page to explore my current projects and discover fascinating insights about exoplanets. You can also check out my ongoing work on Github and see the latest developments in exoplanet data analysis. For a comprehensive overview of my background and experience, please have a look at my curriculum vitae.

I am also associated with the Data Observatory in Chile, where I previously worked.
